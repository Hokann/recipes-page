Chicken Quesadilla

Description

A quesadilla is a cooked tortilla (corn or flour) filled with mainly cheese, but meat, cooked vegetables and spices can be added
aswell. In this recipe, we'll be making a chicken quesadilla with some cooked veggies. This dish is extremely simple, cheap, and
fast to make. Perfect for students! The quesadilla is a famous mexican dish, that is served across the world.

Many variations of the original quesadilla have been created by using different ingredients such as goat cheese, or beans. More
outlandish variations have also surfaced, such as the pizzadilla, the breakfast quesadilla or even the dessert quesadilla.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breast, diced
  • 1 (1.27 ounce) packet fajita seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 green and 2 red bell peppers, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 10 flour tortillas
  • 1 package shredded Cheddar cheese and 1 package shredded Monterey Jack cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat the broiler. Lightly grease a baking sheet.

  2. Toss chicken with fajita seasoning, then spread onto the prepared baking sheet.
    Place under the broiler and cook until chicken is cooked through and no longer
    pink in the center, about 5 minutes.

  3. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).

  4. Heat oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Stir in bell peppers, onion, and
    broiled chicken. Cook and stir until vegetables have softened, about 10 minutes.

  5. Layer half of each tortilla with chicken and vegetable mixture, Cheddar cheese,
    Monterey Jack cheese, and bacon bits. Fold tortillas in half and place onto a baking sheet.

  6. Bake quesadillas in the preheated oven until cheeses have melted, about 10 minutes.
    Cut each quesadilla into wedges and serve.
